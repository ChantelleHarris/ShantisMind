About Me

 He called me Shanti, and that, I was so named. Perhaps I bring him peace, or maybe it serves as a nickname for my birth name, Chantelle. Either way, I accepted and was grateful for it.
 Shanti is the Sanskrit word for peace, and it suits me well, for it's a word that closely desbribes my ongoing emotional state, aura, and personality. At least, that is what I strive for. Every personal thing that I do is a quest for peace, freedom, and creative expression.
 I believe in the sacredness of names, and it means a great deal to me that someone took note of me, beyond me, and called me Shanti. Others have called me Imani Shante, Baccha, and Amira. I find it quite interesting and fortunate that my given name, Chantelle Marie du Jardin, becomes me completely--each name, each meaning. However, I call myself Chantelle Willow Spirit, so named after the grand willow tree and the upper air, the aether.
 I stand grounded to the Earth in love and harmony. Yet, my thoughts and my voice travel in the wind. In this way, I am ever changing--ever growing. Mutable me. I am one who can travel to distant worlds, in and out of time. I am an ear, a voice of wisdom, a care-giver, and nurturer to those fated to visit. For this, I am grateful, and the thought alone has brought me peace.
 Childhood presented me with many fears and challenges to overcome, and that drew me into myself, into thought, into imagination, and into books. I was drawn to poetry and story-telling.
 Notice the video I chose for the background of this page. Can you imagine the serenity experienced by the stones? The moss? Feel the stillness, the rest, the calm, and take a deep breath..
 I can smell damp soil, cool and comforting to the touch, beneath my bare feet. There's a river nearby and a chill in the air that reveals the warmth on my breath. The sun is here, high and bright, peaking through the trees. I find a place in the sunlight, for there is something magical and mystical about standing in a light between two shadows. With eyes closed and breath deep, I silently rejoice in the mixture of hot sun and cool air. I take note of my quickened heartbeat, slowed mind, and the electric charge beneath me. I stand here in mountain pose, alert and strong, grounded to the Earth, surrounded by love, enveloped in Nature's Quiet, the pure and natural symphony of frequencies that frees souls.
 This is why I am here in the wildnerness all alone--to learn to be free and at peace with myself and others. I do love people, and I can, it seems, only connect with them through the power of words. Words, faithful and true, are my superpower, and I share with you now a poem close to my heart, one that will indeed be published on The Wow Journal. Yes, I can, and often do, break my own rules. Do enjoy.

The Cursed Not, Poetic Heart
He said he was "cursed with poetry at a young age," like me, and so developed "unrealistic expectations" of love and romance. But he is alive, and the words that molded his mind--our minds--were written by the flesh and heart, like mine. And so I was called to poetry. I am able to love like them, like him. It cannot be unrealistic. What a terrible grave to dig for the intensity in which we love, we poets. Are we, all of us, doomed to wander alone in the isolation of time periods and memories past? To live breathing in the air of mystery, never having fully loved or been loved? To carry a heart ever ablaze that intensifies the fire of the soul? Oh, separation is death. Our fire is too much to bear alone, and so we are consumed by it--detaching from all, suffering fleeting infatuations throughout the ages.
